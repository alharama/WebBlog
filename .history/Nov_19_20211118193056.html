Brief History of Web Developement

The history of web development has a realtively short timeline as this age of technology has just began to explode recently. We have great tools such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, etc... but those were only recent inventions. We first take a look at the important platform needed for any website, the internet. In the 1960s computer scientists at the US Department of Defense worked with ARPANET to attempt to put together a project like the internet. In 1965. MIT created computers that were able to communicate with eathother, jumpstarting a new revolutionary technology. Some of the first programming languages used were COBOL and BASIC. In the 70s and 80s the internet and web development began to speed up introducing more languages such as Pascal, C, Perl, C++, etc..

The birth of Open-Source Systems in the 1990s was the stepping stone of modern web pages when HTML was created by Tim-Berners-Lee. Open-source projects began to take over with the creation of the Linux operating system and additional programming languages such as Javascript, Python, Haskell, etc.... With tools such as HTML and JavaScript available, the internet began to take over the 2000s with many big name websites. Web Developement is still relatively young, but is continuously changing everyday. It was interesting to see the timeline of the tools we use everyday in class!
